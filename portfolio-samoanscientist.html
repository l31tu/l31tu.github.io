Tālofa lava 👋

My name is Leitu Bonnici and I am a graphic designer, type design and researcher based between Naarm and the Netherlands.

This is a portfolio of some of my previous work. Please use the down arrows to navigate the pages and click through the images to see more of each project.

‘Afa‘afakasi

Various printed matter for ongoing multidisciplinary artwork ‘Afa‘afakasi.

The Approach publication

Publication design for an exhibition text response, the layout resembling a thesis.

An Archaeologist's Dream publication

Publication design for an exhibition response in the form of poetry.

Brothers, Fathers and Uncles publication

Publication of paintings by Devi Seetharam with text by Celine Saoud.

Oceanian essay re-designs

Re-design of influential Oceanian essays starting with ‘Towards a New Oceania’ by Albert Wendt and ‘Our Sea of Islands’ by Epeli Hau‘ofa.

Hyper-Personalised Bookshelves catalogue

The accompanying catalogue for a series of hyper-personalised bookshelves highlighting a clinical approach to object characterisation.

Not a Holiday Brag Book

A publication documenting a semester in Italy, recounting the experience by datafying periods of time, focusing on specific memories and comparing viewpoints.

Indigenous Arctic Futurisms

Proposed publication design containing several text and image works relating to the themes of Arctic Indigeneity, forms of relationality, and futures.

MA Critical Fashion Practices Graduation Show identity

Poster and brochure design for the 2025 MA Critical Fashion Practices Graduation Show.

Le Phem Era website

A simple website for an on-going practice for simple navigation and to let the wide variety of projects be displayed without distraction.

Nic Brunsdon Architects website

Proposed website design and development for a Boorloo/Perth based architecture studio.

(Non-institutional) Corresponence

A publication comprising a series of posters created with 35 collaborators. Marked by exchanges of learning and support, project showcases the value of education outside of institutional spaces where harm is enabled and knowledges are limited.

Joseph Chuchward Typefaces

A number of typefaces in development including digitisations and interpretations of alphabets by Samoan-born type designer, Joseph Churchward.

Colonial Typefaces

A series of typefaces that takes back resources from colonial cultures referencing German, American and English complicity in the colonisation of Sāmoa.

Cultural Typefaces

Two very Samoan typefaces in development—the first in collaboration with artist Peter Lemalu representing the fractured experience of living in the diaspora—and the second—a typeface designed for individual characters to be used in isolation as graphic elements.
